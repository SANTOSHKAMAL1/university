Student Mentoring Training Program
28 May 2024 - 29 June 2024 10 Days Training Bengaluru / Kochi Campuses
Mentoring Faculty Development Student Support

Program Overview:

The Student Mentoring Training Program was designed to familiarize faculty members with the process of mentoring. Originally planned as a two-day program, it was tailored to a one-day workshop without diluting the impact.

Key Statistics:

Faculty Enrolled

872

Faculty Participated

780

Sessions Conducted

10

Key Takeaways:

  • Participants enjoyed activity-oriented learning rather than pure classroom sessions
  • Case studies and games were more impactful for participation-oriented workshops
  • Faculty members understood mentoring concepts, processes, and their impact on student development
  • Training was related to what faculty members do, making it practical and understandable

Evaluation & Feedback:

  • Two evaluations conducted by VC and CAO immediately after the program
  • Progress presentation made to VC and CAO on 18 Oct 2024
  • Feedback from participants was encouraging
  • Major challenges are in implementing the process
Capacity Building Workshops 2024-25

Workshop Themes:

Mentoring Td-PCL OBE MOOC Development NEP 2020

Key Objectives:

  • Enhance faculty competencies in innovative pedagogical approaches
  • Build capacity for Outcome-Based Education (OBE)
  • Equip faculty with mentoring skills for student support
  • Promote Transdisciplinary Project-Centric Learning (Td-PCL)
  • Develop capabilities in MOOC design and development
  • Foster continuous professional development

Impact & Coverage:

  • Universal Coverage: Across all 28 departments and 7 Faculties
  • Structured Reporting: Detailed post-workshop reports with feedback analysis
  • MOOC Development: 23 MOOCs developed and live on SWAYAM platform
  • Faculty Induction: Month-long FIP conducted twice every academic year
  • Malaviya Mission: 66 faculty registrations in May 2025, 37 in June 2025

Future Plans (2025-2028):

  • Professional Development Series with industry experts
  • Scale up MOOC development efforts
  • Expand Transdisciplinary Project-Centric Learning
  • Advanced training on curriculum mapping and assessment design
  • Institutionalize mentoring with digital tracking systems
  • Outbound training programs for interpersonal skills development
